# Counsel Opinion — Safe-Reversal / Restore Spine (Data · Schema · Deploy)

- **Status:** COUNSEL (advisory) — Triadic Council STEP 2 (examine). Non-blocking except the two ETHICAL-STOPs below, which are *friction requiring a recorded human decision*, not vetoes. The human is final. The **urgent first backup (Ordering step 0)** is never gated on this document — a system with zero backups and leaked prod creds should not wait on a philosopher.
- **Examined:** `proposal.md`, `ADR-safe-reversal-spine.md`, `AUDIT-SYNTHESIS-2026-07-03.md` (§2 LC7, §3 R-C), `recon3-privacy-ops-2026-07-03.md` (P-H1, P-M2, P-L2, O-H1/2/6/7, O-M4/8), `docs/backup/runbooks.md`, `compliance/RoPA.md`, the secrets-incident memory (`secrets-exposure-incident-2026-07-03`). Grounded against the CLAUDE.md Ethics Charter + the Context-Handoff red-lines.
- **Posture:** the engineering is disciplined and the architect *surfaced* the hard questions rather than burying them — §1.6, open-Q3, open-Q4, and the "drill that fails when reversal is broken" principle are exactly the right instincts. This opinion answers the questions handed to Counsel forcefully; it does not accuse. Robustness (canary partial-restore, break-old-image-without-migrations enumeration — open-Q1/Q2) is the **Breaker's lane** and is not duplicated here.

## 1. Reasoning by lenses (only what adds signal)

### Justice / whose cost, whose harm
- **This is corrective justice for everyone at once.** A system that can silently, permanently lose every customer's order, every courier's owed settlement, and every owner's business — with the docs *claiming* a recovery net that does not exist (`disaster-recovery.md` vs LC7) — harms all three stakeholder classes and hides the harm behind a false promise. Fixing it is unambiguous. No hesitation on the core.
- **The absent party is the erased data subject.** Every other actor in a 3am disaster-restore has a voice in the room: the operator executes, the owner is alerted, couriers/customers complain. The one person with a stake and **no representation** is the customer who exercised Art.17 and asked to be forgotten — a restore silently resurrects their home GPS, their doorway photo, and their messenger handle (the P-H1 estate). Nobody speaks for them at restore time. **The only structural advocate they can have is a blocking re-erasure step in the restore script itself** (ESC-1). A runbook line is not an advocate; it is the first thing skipped under an RTO clock.
- **The retention decision costs a party who never consented to the cost.** A 13-month full-PII monthly snapshot is a *storage-limitation* decision. Per the RoPA, for customer order data dowiz is the **processor** and the owner is the **controller** (`RoPA.md:9,11,12`). A platform-default retention is therefore the processor quietly deciding a duty (Art.5(1)(e)) that legally belongs to the controller — and the data subject bears the residual-exposure cost of that decision (ESC-2).

### Dignity / autonomy
- **The destructive-restore human gate is designed, and I affirm it.** `pg_restore --clean --if-exists` over live prod is irreversible; A-4's prod-host guard + `--i-am-restoring-prod` + **typed target confirmation** honors "human-in-the-loop / zero-autopilot" for the one truly destructive power in the spine. Critically, **no path auto-restores the DB** — §3.3 auto-rollback is *image only*, and migration-bearing failures get "alert + manual runbook," never an automated data restore. The dangerous automated path simply does not exist. This is the correct shape. (One enrichment in §3: the confirmation should also *show the data-loss cost* of the restore, so the human's consent is informed, not merely typed.)
- **`down()` demoted is a dignity-of-the-operator win.** Removing 157 untested inverses so no exhausted operator reaches for a `migrate:down` that will `SET NOT NULL` on legitimately-nulled anonymized rows (M16) protects the human from a trap the system itself laid. Naming it explicitly in `rollback.md` is correct.

### Honesty / consent / no false claim
- **This spine's deepest virtue is that it kills a lie.** The current docs assert a recovery net that has *never once been able to pass* (LC7, C2). "Worse than absence" is the architect's own phrase and it is exactly right — an operator reaching for a net that isn't there is more endangered than one who knows they have none. Every mechanism here ships with a drill that *fails when reversal is broken*, which converts a doc-claim into a monitored signal. This is aesthetics-as-ethics realized: the honest design and the safe design are the same design.
- **But the re-erasure claim is not yet honest enough.** "Post-restore re-application of completed erasures" (ADR line 67) re-invokes the **currently-broken** erasure path (P-H1: the erasure that leaves GPS + door photo + handle in place). Re-applying a broken erasure after a restore produces the *appearance* of honoring the subject while leaving the most sensitive data resurrected — the C2 false-confidence disease reborn in the privacy domain. Honesty demands the re-erasure step **assert its post-condition** (the subject's sensitive fields are actually null) or fail loud (ESC-1).
- **The retention number is honest but the tier structure hides the real question.** hourly-24h / daily-30d / weekly-90d are self-justifying disaster-recovery necessity — bless them. The **monthly-13-month tier is not DR** (you never recover a disaster from a year-old snapshot; disasters restore from the newest good backup). Its purpose is archival/point-in-time-a-year-ago, which is precisely the purpose that needs an affirmative legal basis — and it is the tier that holds resurrectable PII the *longest*. Putting the friction on ">13 months" (ADR line 66) is aiming slightly past the target; the friction belongs on *why the monthly tier exists at all* (ESC-2).

### Care / harm — proportionality of the fail-safe folds
- **Snapshot-upload-failure-aborts-the-release is the right fail-safe** (§2.4): old code keeps serving, no one is harmed, a human deploys later. Failing *toward* the safe state, not the dangerous one. Endorsed.
- **But a pre-migrate-snapshot restore is not free, and the care-cost is undocumented.** Restoring the pre-migrate snapshot on a live system *loses every write between the migration and the restore* — real orders placed and paid in that window vanish. RPO≈0 *relative to the snapshot* is honest, but the operator must weigh "roll forward and repair N orders" vs "restore and *lose* N orders" with that cost in front of them. Right now the runbook implies restore is a clean undo; it is a trade. Name it (§3).

### Long horizon / reversibility / strategy
- **Serves the launch trigger correctly.** You cannot honestly take a first real paid order on a system that could permanently lose that order with no recovery. This is a *precondition* for taking money, not polish. Correctly prioritized.
- **Portability is honored, and that is strategically load-bearing.** Self-managed pg_dump→R2 works against any Postgres and any S3-compatible store; Supabase PITR is provider-locked. Making A primary *because* it is portable (not merely cheaper) aligns the recovery spine with the ADR-020 open-source direction. The portable mechanism is also the blast-radius-independent one and the drillable one — three virtues converging is a sign of a real design, not a rationalization.
- **The true recurring cost is operator *attention*, not dollars — and that is the system's actual bottleneck.** "$0.15/mo" is honest about R2 and dishonest-by-omission about the real tax: a self-managed chain is paid for in the solo operator's attention (watch the drills, hold the keys, execute the restore under stress). For a one-human operation, attention is scarcer than $25/mo. This reframes the B-Pro decision (§3 advice + steel-man).

### Aesthetic / conceptual integrity
- **"Every layer = mechanism + a drill that fails when it's broken" is a genuinely elegant spine** — one principle, applied three times, self-similar. Preserve it as a first-class invariant, not an implementation detail. It is the design-language the rest of the system should be held to.
- **"Schema rich, runtime minimal" holds:** ~30 MB image growth, seconds of release latency, no new infra — the cost is concentrated in scripts and drills. Restraint is real here.

### Epistemic — carrying assumption / absent perspective
- **Load-bearing unchecked assumption:** the re-erasure step assumes a *working* erasure. It does not exist yet (P-H1) and it strands on transient failure (LC4). The spine declares GDPR-erasure fixes a non-goal — fair scoping — but then *depends on* that non-goal for its own privacy correctness. That dependency must be made explicit and sequenced, or the spine ships a promise it cannot keep (ESC-1).
- **A second unchecked assumption:** the three drills prove the *machine* can restore; none proves *this operator* can execute the full RTO path (provision fresh Supabase + secrets cutover + DNS + verify) under real pressure. The scratch-DB drill deliberately avoids touching prod — correct for safety — but it means the dominant RTO terms (detection + human execution, per §1.7) have **never been rehearsed by the actual human**. RTO ≤4h is a spreadsheet claim, not a rehearsed capability (§3 advice).
- **Absent perspective:** the operator's *successor*. Both key-escrow stores are operator-held. If the operator is incapacitated, the backups become permanently-undecryptable ciphertext and no one can run a restore — a total-loss continuity gap that the "3-2-1, two independent stores" framing papers over (§3 advice + open question).

## 2. ETHICAL-STOPs (grounded red-line crossings only — friction, human decides, non-blocking of the urgent first backup)

### ESC-1 — Restore-time re-erasure must be a *blocking, post-condition-asserted* step, and its dependency on the broken erasure path must be confronted

- **Grounded line:** "анонімізувати-не-видаляти" (a restore that silently un-does a completed erasure is de-facto un-anonymization) + human-final/recorded-decision + Ethics Charter ("serve human wellbeing, dignity… never turned against the people it was learned from" — a subject who withdrew their data has it turned back on, resurrected from cold storage).
- **The crossing:** as written, re-erasure is a **runbook line** (open-Q3 concedes this and asks Counsel the question). Under an RTO clock, a stressed solo operator skips or forgets a runbook line — so a restore silently resurrects the P-H1 estate (home GPS, doorway photo, messenger handle) and it *stays* resurrected. Worse: the re-erasure it would run re-invokes the **currently-broken** erasure worker (P-H1), so even when executed it leaves the most sensitive fields in place — false comfort, the C2 disease in the privacy domain.
- **What discharges it (minimal, proportional):**
  1. Re-erasure of completed `gdpr_erasure_requests` becomes a **non-skippable step of the restore path** (script, not runbook), that **logs** what it re-erased and **asserts its post-condition** (the named subjects' `delivery_lat/lng`, `delivery_photo_key` + R2 object, `messenger_handle` are actually null/gone) — a restore that cannot re-erase is **incomplete**, not "green," by the same "drill fails when reversal is broken" principle the spine already lives by;
  2. the design **records the dependency on the P-H1 erasure fix** and either sequences after it or *fails loud* (the restore reports "erasure re-application UNVERIFIED" rather than silently succeeding);
  3. one recorded human decision: *"we accept restore-completes-without-verified-re-erasure, OR we gate restore-completion on it."* Nobody-decides must not default to resurrect-and-keep.
- **Plurality behind the demand:** deontological (the subject withdrew consent; resurrecting their data has no legal basis); care (the maximally-sensitive data — a photo of someone's home — is exactly what a restore leaks); corrective justice (honor the erasure you promised); consequentialist (a resurrected-and-retained door photo is a concrete dignity harm to an identifiable person).
- **Scope discipline:** does **not** block Ordering step 0 (a single first artifact, not the automated ladder). It attaches to *restore-path closure* and to the *automated cadence going live*, never to taking the first backup.

### ESC-2 — The full-PII retention ladder (esp. the monthly tier) is a *controller* storage-limitation decision that must be recorded with a legal basis and surfaced in the DPA, not shipped as a silent platform default

- **Grounded line:** storage-limitation / "анонімізувати-не-видаляти" sibling (retaining resurrectable PII longer than justified *is* over-retention) + honesty ("records tell the truth" — a legal basis that lives only in a config constant tells no one) + human-final (a recorded controller decision, not a default nobody signed).
- **The crossing:** for customer order data, dowiz is the **processor** and the owner is the **controller** (`RoPA.md:9,11,12`). A hard-coded 13-month retention on full-PII dumps is the *processor* deciding the *controller's* Art.5(1)(e) duty by default. And the honest structural fact the proposal hasn't stated: the DR artifact is **one co-mingled dump of all tenants** — so *per-controller retention is impossible*; the processor unavoidably imposes one number on everyone. That makes disclosure and a recorded basis *more* necessary, not less. Finally, the monthly-13mo tier is **not disaster recovery** (disasters restore from the newest backup) — it is the archival tier, the one that outlives DR necessity and holds resurrectable PII the longest, and it currently has no stated purpose beyond "down from 7y."
- **What discharges it (minimal, proportional):**
  1. **Bless hourly-24h / daily-30d / weekly-90d as self-justifying DR** — no friction; these are operational necessity;
  2. record an explicit **controller-level decision** (operator acting as/for the controller) stating the **legal basis and purpose of the monthly tier specifically**, and surface the retention term in the **DPA-with-owners** (`compliance/contracts/dpa-with-owners-template.md`) as a documented processing instruction, not an invisible constant;
  3. **13 months for the monthly tier is defensible** *if* the recorded basis is "annual-cycle / dispute / audit" — I bless it at that value, not shorter, *conditioned on the basis being written*. But if the 7-year figure was really a **statutory financial-records** obligation, that obligation must move to a **separate, minimized, purpose-scoped financial archive** (invoices/settlements/tax — no GPS, no door photos), **not** be met by keeping full-PII DB dumps. Conflating "keep financial records 7y" with "keep the whole PII-bearing database 7y" is a category error the 7→13 cut half-fixes; naming it finishes the fix.
- **Plurality:** deontological (storage-limitation is a duty, not a preference); justice (the data subject bears the exposure cost of a retention nobody recorded); honesty (a legal basis must be legible to survive an audit); care (shorter retention of resurrectable PII = smaller blast radius on the next incident).
- **Scope discipline:** does **not** block step 0. Gates the *automated ladder with the monthly tier*.

*(Not escalated to ESC: the destructive full-restore gate — it is **already designed** with typed confirmation + prod-host guard + no automated DB restore. A red-line that the proposal already respects needs affirmation and one enrichment, not a STOP. Handled in §3. The credential-sequencing concern is real and high-priority but is security-hygiene, not one of my grounded red-lines — I keep it as advice #1, not an inflated ESC.)*

## 3. Non-blocking advice (strategic · aesthetic · sequencing)

1. **Sequence rotation BEFORE Ordering step 0 — and do not re-open the leak with a throwaway credential script.** Step 0 (first backup *today*) is right and urgent *precisely because* the prod superuser creds are leaked (`secrets-exposure-incident-2026-07-03`): an attacker with those creds can DROP the DB before rotation, and there is no net. But three sequencing constraints make it safe, not reckless:
   - **Rotate the leaked superuser + api-user passwords FIRST** (Supabase dashboard, minutes — already remediation #1 and independent of everything). Take the first-ever backup under the *rotated* credential, closing the leaked window in the same motion.
   - **Use a read-only backup role, not the (rotated) superuser** — pg_dump needs SELECT only. The ADR already adopts the A′ read-only-role idea "immediately"; make step 0 the first user of it, so no superuser credential is ever handled on a workstation at all.
   - **Do not write a new throwaway script.** The leak class *was* throwaway prod-cred scripts in `.agents/tmp/`. Make step 0 exercise the *same* hardened path being built (credential via env/`.pgpass` only — never argv, never the manifest, never a log line; the script gitignored, or better, it *is* the restore-CLI code, not an orphan). Re-spawning a debug script re-walks into the exact trap that just bit the project.
   This is friction, not a blocker — the first backup should still happen this week.

2. **Buy the Supabase-Pro belt SOONER than "at first revenue."** The proposal defers B-Pro to first revenue and PITR to "when >1h loss is commercially unacceptable." Reframe by the actual bottleneck — *operator attention*. $25/mo buys a **provider-run, zero-operator-attention** second net (daily backup + auto-pause removal) that survives a botched or un-executed self-managed drill. For a solo operator whose attention is the single point of failure, that is cheap insurance to hold *before* first revenue, layered on the self-managed floor. Self-managed = the blast-radius-independent floor; Pro = the attention-independent belt. Hold both early.

3. **Add a fourth, human drill: an annual game-day.** The three drills prove the *machine* restores; none proves *this operator* can execute the full RTO path (provision + secrets cutover + DNS + verify) under pressure — the dominant RTO terms in §1.7 are human and unrehearsed. Once a year, have the operator execute the **entire** disaster path against a throwaway target, timed, from the runbook. This extends "a reversal path never drilled is a hope" to the human part of reversal — the part the current drills structurally exclude. If burden forces a cut, the **minimum-viable priority is: daily data-restore signal > quarterly snapshot drill > weekly deploy drill** (guard the irreversible first; deploys are fix-forwardable). Cut in that order, never randomly.

4. **Solve the bus-factor of one on the escrow key.** "Two independent stores" (SOPS/age vault + operator password manager) is **store**-independent, not **custodian**-independent — one human holds both, possibly on one laptop, in one head. If the operator is incapacitated, the backups are permanently-undecryptable ciphertext and no one can restore. Add a documented **break-glass third copy** in a form a trusted second party can reach (sealed envelope with a lawyer/co-founder, safe-deposit) with a written procedure. This is a care/continuity duty to the customers and couriers whose recoverability currently hangs entirely on one person's availability.

5. **Show the data-loss cost at the restore confirmation, and name it for pre-migrate restores.** The destructive gate is sound; enrich it so the human's consent is *informed*: at `--i-am-restoring-prod` time, surface *what will be lost* (backup age / writes-since). For the pre-migrate-snapshot restore specifically, `rollback.md` must state plainly that restoring loses every order placed between the migration and the restore — so the operator consciously chooses "roll forward and repair" vs "restore and lose N orders," rather than believing restore is a clean undo.

6. **Aesthetic endorsement — protect the spine's one principle.** "Mechanism + a drill that fails when reversal is broken," applied self-similarly at all three layers, is the elegant core. Do not let anyone collapse a layer to mechanism-without-drill "to ship faster" — the drill *is* the mechanism's honesty. Likewise keep A-then-B (fix the portable chain, then optionally add the managed belt); do not let it collapse to B-only, which would trade the portable/drillable/blast-independent floor for provider convenience.

## 4. Steel-man of a rejected option — Supabase PITR as the *primary* net

The proposal rejects PITR-as-primary/sole for same-account blast radius, prod-only coverage, non-drillability, ~$100/mo, and ADR-020 portability conflict. The strongest case *for* it, from a lens the proposal underweights (care for the humans-in-the-loop + the data subject):

- **The scarcest resource is the operator's attention, and PITR spends dollars to conserve it.** The self-managed chain's entire cost is attention — hold the keys, watch the drills, and *hand-execute a 30–55-minute pg_restore path at 3am, under stress, for the first time for real* despite scratch-drills. The person most likely to fumble a disaster restore is a solo human doing it once, live. PITR is a provider-tested dashboard button: it removes the human-error surface of the hand-executed path — the surface most likely to *cause* a second disaster during recovery.
- **PITR is strictly more protective of the data subjects in the loop.** RPO ~2 min vs up to 1h means a disaster loses ~2 minutes of orders, not up to an hour — dramatically fewer customers with a placed-but-vanished order and fewer couriers with a lost settlement. On the *care* lens, PITR's tighter RPO is a direct reduction in human harm, not a convenience.
- **"Not drillable into a scratch target" cuts both ways.** True — you cannot run the round-trip proof against PITR. But you also cannot *fumble* it: there is no operator-authored restore script to have a bug, no key to lose, no grants to strip (H10). The self-managed chain trades provider-tested reliability for drillability; the steel-man says drillability matters *because* the self-managed path is fragile, whereas PITR needs less drilling *because* it is less fragile.

**Honest rebuttal (why the proposal's choice is still correct):** the leaked-creds incident makes "attacker deletes the Supabase project *and* its provider backups in one motion" a live scenario — provider backups die with the account, so PITR-as-*sole*-net fails the exact threat that is live today. It covers prod only (staging is Fly PG). And as sole net it contradicts ADR-020 portability. These are decisive *against sole*.

**Synthesis (my actual recommendation, not either/or):** the steel-man does not overturn A-primary; it **pulls B-Pro/PITR forward in time.** The self-managed chain is the blast-radius-independent, portable floor — keep it primary. But the human-error-under-pressure cost and the data-subject RPO cost of the self-managed restore are real *from order #1*, not just "at volume." Adopt the managed belt earlier than the proposal's thresholds, explicitly *as a human-error-and-RPO reducer*, not merely "when budget allows." (This is the same conclusion advice #2 reaches from the attention-bottleneck angle — two lenses, one nudge.)

## 5. One open question no one asked

**Does a disaster-restore that *transiently resurrects* an erased subject's data — even for the minutes before re-erasure runs — itself constitute processing without legal basis, and therefore a potential personal-data-breach notification event (Art.33/34)?**

The entire chain — audit, synthesis, ADR, proposal — treats re-erasure as a *cleanup* that makes the restore acceptable. But between `pg_restore` completing and re-erasure asserting done, the withdrawn data of a person who exercised Art.17 is *live in the production database again*, queryable, notifiable, exportable. The subject withdrew their legal basis; the restore re-instantiates their PII without one, however briefly. Nobody asked whether that window is (a) merely an operational step to minimize, or (b) a reportable event — a resurrection of data a subject had the right to have gone. The answer shapes ESC-1's stringency: if transient resurrection is itself a breach-class event, then re-erasure must not only be *blocking* but *fast and logged as a breach-mitigation*, and the affected subjects may be owed a re-notification ("your erased data was briefly restored during an incident and re-erased at HH:MM"). It should be run down with whatever counsel advises the controller *before* the automated cadence goes live, because it may convert re-erasure from a courtesy into a documented obligation — and because the subject who cannot be in the 3am room deserves to know their withdrawal was, for a few minutes, undone.

## Verdict

**PROCEED-WITH-REVISIONS.** The spine is honest, portable, elegantly principled, and fixes the single scariest ops gap in the system; the destructive-restore human gate is sound; the drill-driven design structurally fights the false-confidence class it exists to kill. Ship the urgent first backup (step 0) this week — after rotating the leaked creds and using a read-only role. Before the *automated ladder* goes live, discharge ESC-1 (blocking, post-condition-asserted re-erasure, with the P-H1 dependency confronted) and ESC-2 (recorded controller-level legal basis for the monthly retention tier, DPA-surfaced).
