# Trust & Likeability Playbook for dowiz — winning skeptical small food vendors in Albania

**Date:** 2026-07-03 · **Type:** product + growth research (web-sourced, cited)
**Context:** dowiz/DeliveryOS is a vendor-owned, 0%-commission ordering + delivery OS (QR/link storefront at `/s/:slug`, own courier fleet, cash/crypto, Telegram/WhatsApp/Viber comms, self-hostable, AGPL). Target: a small food vendor in Albania who has never heard of it — low-tech, cash-heavy, relationship-driven, operating in a ~28% informal economy.

---

## 0. The market reality that shapes everything below

Three facts about the Albanian small-vendor context override generic SaaS playbooks:

1. **Cash is the economy.** Albanian businesses receive ~99% of payment volume in cash; >80% of all transactions are cash; card/POS penetration is the lowest in the region ([AAB / Bankieri](https://issuu.com/aabshshb/docs/bankieri_34_eng_per_web/s/10165901), [CPT Group](https://thecptgroup.com/cash-system-spotlight-albania/)). CGAP research on micro-merchant digitization adds a critical nuance: merchants often *avoid* digital payment tools because visibility of turnover threatens their informal status, and trust in any digital offer is contingent on **not disrupting their existing cash flow** ([CGAP: Digitizing Merchant Payments](https://www.cgap.org/research/publication/digitizing-merchant-payments-why-and-how), [PLOS One: Jaipur merchants study](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371%2Fjournal.pone.0219450)). → **dowiz's cash-on-delivery-first design is not a compromise — it is the #1 trust feature.** Never lead with digital payments; lead with "orders come in, cash stays yours, nothing about how you get paid changes."

2. **Relationships and word of mouth are the channel.** Word of mouth is by far the most trusted recommendation form; referred customers stay 37% longer with 16% higher LTV ([Invesp](https://www.invespcro.com/blog/word-of-mouth-marketing/)); B2B decision makers trust peer insights (55%) over the vendor's own website ([Trustmary](https://trustmary.com/social-proof/how-trust-elements-impact-b2b-buying-decisions/)). Slice built the largest US independent-pizzeria network on exactly this: 900+ local reps, "Slice is a trust business… I call my Slice rep and they get it — because they know pizza shops" ([SaaStr on Slice](https://www.saastr.com/the-secrets-to-selling-and-scaling-in-vertical-saas-with-slices-cro-loren-padelford/), [Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/janetforgrieve/2021/04/15/how-slice-is-empowering-local-pizzerias-in-the-digital-age/)).

3. **The wedge already exists: commission resentment.** Independent restaurants run 3–9% net margins while marketplace apps (Glovo/Wolt/Bolt Food/Baboon in Albania) take 15–30% per order — the platform's cut exceeds the vendor's margin on the meal ([LAist](https://laist.com/news/food/why-so-many-restaurants-hate-food-delivery-apps), [CloudKitchens](https://cloudkitchens.com/blog/delivery-app-fees)). ChowNow turned "0% commission" into its whole identity and a running "$470M saved for restaurants" counter ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/chow-now-sticks-to-zero-commission-orders-as-hits-keep-coming-for-restaurants-162732866.html)). Albania's marketplaces also earn mixed reviews (delays, stale menus — see [Baboon on Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=al.baboon)), so *reliability itself* is a differentiator.

One more channel fact: **Viber, not email, is the Balkans' default communication utility** — #1 in several Balkan countries with 90%+ smartphone penetration in its strongholds ([Sinch: most popular messaging apps by country](https://sinch.com/blog/most-popular-messaging-apps-by-country/), [Infobip](https://www.infobip.com/blog/most-popular-messaging-apps-by-country)); WhatsApp usage among emerging-market SMBs is ~80%+ ([Wapikit WhatsApp stats](https://www.wapikit.com/blog/global-whatsapp-business-statistics-2025)). dowiz already ships Telegram/WhatsApp/Viber comms — this is a native advantage; email-based anything is a dead letter here.

## 1. TRUST playbook — ranked tactics

> Ranking = expected impact on a skeptical, low-tech, cash-heavy Albanian vendor, weighted by evidence strength and dowiz's existing assets.

### T1. Show the finished product before asking for anything (the claimable demo)
- **Tactic:** Build the vendor's *actual* storefront before first contact — real menu, photos, hours, their name — and open the pitch with "your menu is already online, here's the link; claim it if you want it." dowiz's certified demo-builder loop (prospect → polished claimable `/s/:slug` demo, enriched via Maps scrape) already does exactly this.
- **Why it works:** This is the strongest possible risk reversal — zero effort, zero money, zero data handed over before value is *seen*. Users must hit first value fast or they leave (self-serve benchmark: minutes, not days — [ProductQuant 5-minute-aha](https://productquant.dev/blog/5-minute-aha-rule-optimize-ttv/)); a pre-built demo collapses time-to-value to zero. It also fires the strongest credibility signal: 75% of consumers judge a business's credibility by its website, 94% of first impressions are design-related ([Made For Web](https://www.madeforweb.co.uk/blog/75-of-consumers-judge-a-companys-credibility-by-its-site-design), [Zippia website statistics](https://www.zippia.com/advice/website-statistics/)) — the vendor sees *their own shop looking professional* and imagines their customers seeing it.
- **dowiz implementation:** Productize the demo-builder loop as the default acquisition motion. Claim CTA on every demo storefront ("Ky është dyqani juaj? Merreni falas" — "Is this your shop? Claim it free"). Claim flow ≤ 3 fields, phone-number based (no email), instantly hands over admin.
- **Effort:** S–M (loop exists; needs claim-CTA polish + batch tooling).

### T2. Cash-first, "nothing changes about your money" positioning
- **Tactic:** Explicitly promise: cash on delivery stays default, no POS required, no bank account required, no forced digital payments, dowiz never touches or routes the vendor's money.
- **Why it works:** CGAP: micro-merchant trust in a digital offer is contingent on it *not disrupting existing cash transactions*; merchants resist tools that expose turnover ([CGAP](https://www.cgap.org/research/publication/digitizing-merchant-payments-why-and-how), [CGAP: Breaking the Cash Habit](https://www.cgap.org/blog/breaking-cash-habit-urgent-need-to-accelerate-digital-merchant-payments)). In a country where businesses take ~99% of payment volume in cash ([Bankieri](https://issuu.com/aabshshb/docs/bankieri_34_eng_per_web/s/10165901)), any hint of "payments platform" reads as risk (bank friction + tax visibility). dowiz's cash-as-proof delivery flow is already built around this reality.
- **dowiz implementation:** Landing + pitch line: "Porositë vijnë online. Paratë mbeten cash, në dorën tuaj." ("Orders come online. Money stays cash, in your hand.") FAQ item addressing the fear directly. Keep card/crypto strictly opt-in and visually secondary everywhere.
- **Effort:** S (copy + positioning; the product already works this way).

### T3. 0% commission with a personal savings counter
- **Tactic:** Make "0% commission, forever" the loudest claim, and make it *concrete per vendor*: a calculator ("At 100 orders/month averaging 1,500 lekë, Glovo-style 25% commission costs you 37,500 lekë/month — dowiz costs you 0") plus a live "you kept X lekë this month" counter in the admin.
- **Why it works:** The commission wound is the single most documented pain of small food vendors — platform cut exceeds their entire net margin ([LAist](https://laist.com/news/food/why-so-many-restaurants-hate-food-delivery-apps), [CloudKitchens](https://cloudkitchens.com/blog/delivery-app-fees)). ChowNow's aggregate "$470M saved" counter is the proven pattern ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/chow-now-sticks-to-zero-commission-orders-as-hits-keep-coming-for-restaurants-162732866.html)); Slice's "as little as 5% vs 20–30%" framing built a 3,000-city network ([Audacy](https://www.audacy.com/wcbs880/news/business/slice-keeps-local-pizzerias-thriving-through-digital-means)). Concrete numbers in the vendor's own currency beat abstract percentages.
- **dowiz implementation:** Landing-page calculator (lekë, local order sizes); admin dashboard "money kept vs. marketplace commission" tile; aggregate "kept by dowiz vendors so far" counter once ≥10 vendors.
- **Effort:** M (one calculator component + one admin tile off existing order totals).

### T4. A human on Viber/WhatsApp, and a face they know
- **Tactic:** Support = a real person on Viber/WhatsApp with a local number, answering in Albanian, same-day. Founder/local rep visits vendors in person for setup and problems. No ticket portal, no email-first support.
- **Why it works:** Viber is the Balkans' default channel ([Sinch](https://sinch.com/blog/most-popular-messaging-apps-by-country/)); 75% of consumers are more likely to buy again when customer care is in their own language ([CSA Research](https://csa-research.com/l/media/Consumers-Prefer-their-Own-Language)). Slice's trust engine is literally "I call my Slice rep, and they get it" ([SaaStr](https://www.saastr.com/the-secrets-to-selling-and-scaling-in-vertical-saas-with-slices-cro-loren-padelford/)). In relationship-driven markets, the person *is* the product's trust layer; CGAP found rural merchant trust was built through in-person workshops and partnerships, not self-serve funnels ([GSMA](https://www.gsma.com/solutions-and-impact/connectivity-for-good/mobile-for-development/blog/mobile-money-adoption-strategies-for-start-ups-in-emerging-markets/)).
- **dowiz implementation:** Viber/WhatsApp support line printed on the landing page, in admin footer, and on the printed QR kit. Founder photo + name + phone on the landing "Kush jemi ne" section. Every early vendor gets an in-person setup visit.
- **Effort:** S tooling / M ongoing (it's an operating commitment, not a feature).

### T5. Named local proof: 3 vendor case studies + review discipline
- **Tactic:** Get 3 Albanian vendors (even free/discounted) and publish named, photographed mini case studies with one number each ("Restorant X, Tirana: 214 porosi muajin e parë, 0 lekë komision"). Show a live vendor count. Respond publicly to every review/comment.
- **Why it works:** Case studies are the top proof format for B2B buyers (73% find them crucial; testimonials lift conversion ~34%) ([Flint trust-signal statistics](https://www.flint.com/blog/landing-page-trust-signal-conversion-statistics)); peers > vendor claims (55%, [Trustmary](https://trustmary.com/social-proof/how-trust-elements-impact-b2b-buying-decisions/)). BrightLocal: 93% read reviews; **88% would use a business that replies to all its reviews vs 47% for one that doesn't**, and review *recency* now matters more than volume ([BrightLocal Local Consumer Review Survey](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey/)). For a vendor in Tirana, one recognizable neighbor on dowiz outweighs any feature list.
- **dowiz implementation:** Landing-page section: 3 cards (photo of the owner in their shop, name, city, one number, one quote in Albanian). Auto-updating "X shitës aktivë" count once the number stops being embarrassing (>10); until then, name the vendors individually instead.
- **Effort:** M (mostly fieldwork: recruiting + photographing the first 3).

### T6. Risk-reversal stack: free start, no card, no contract, leave with your data
- **Tactic:** Bundle every risk reversal into one visible promise block: free to start · no card details · no contract/term · cancel anytime · export your menu + customers + order history in one tap · (and, because it's AGPL) "the software is open — you could even run it yourself."
- **Why it works:** Opt-in trials (no credit card) maximize top-of-funnel entry ([ChartMogul SaaS Conversion Report](https://chartmogul.com/reports/saas-conversion-report/)); risk-reversal language lifts win rates ~32% and money-back guarantees lift sales ~21% via loss-aversion psychology ([SPOTIO](https://spotio.com/blog/risk-reversal-language-punches-your-ticket-to-32-higher-win-rates/), [Textbuilder](https://www.textbuilder.ai/boost-sales-funnel-confidence-with-risk-reversal/)). Open source + data export is the credible backstop for "no lock-in": buyers of open/self-hostable tools consistently cite "can never be held hostage" as the decision driver ([FusionAuth on self-hostable architecture](https://fusionauth.io/blog/building-self-hostable-application), [LogClaw](https://logclaw.ai/blog/self-hosted-observability-open-source-beats-saas)). For a low-tech vendor the AGPL badge itself means nothing — but "your data is yours, take it and go anytime, and the code is public so we *can't* trap you" translates it into their language.
- **dowiz implementation:** One "Pa rrezik" (no-risk) block on landing + pricing pages listing all six promises. One-tap export (menu CSV/JSON + customer list + order history) in admin settings — this must actually exist, not just be claimed. GDPR/export already partially built (owner/gdpr routes) — surface it.
- **Effort:** S–M (copy + wiring existing export endpoints to one visible button).

### T7. Reliability transparency: status page + "no lost orders" promise
- **Tactic:** Public status page (uptime, incidents in plain Albanian), plus an explicit operational promise: an order is never silently lost — every order is confirmed to the customer AND pushed to the vendor on their messaging channel, with a printable fallback.
- **Why it works:** Status pages are a now-standard trust device that also cuts support load ([UptimeRobot guide](https://uptimerobot.com/knowledge-hub/monitoring/building-a-status-page-ultimate-guide/), [Watchman Tower](https://www.watchmantower.com/blog/what-is-a-public-status-page)). For this audience the deeper point: their livelihood is per-order; the local marketplaces are visibly flaky (stale menus, delays — [Baboon reviews](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=al.baboon)); "it just works, and when it doesn't we tell you first" is a trust position competitors don't hold. Missing orders is the one failure that kills the relationship permanently.
- **dowiz implementation:** `/status` page fed by the existing honest health check (audit wave-1). Order fan-out to Telegram/WhatsApp/Viber is the delivery guarantee mechanism — market it ("çdo porosi ju vjen në Viber"). Add the promise to landing + onboarding.
- **Effort:** M (status page S; the promise requires the notification path to be provably reliable — it already has pg-boss retry/DLQ).

### T8. Compliance signals, sized for the audience
- **Tactic:** GDPR-aligned data handling, daily backups, EU data residency — stated in one quiet line, not a compliance wall.
- **Why it works:** For enterprise buyers absent security documentation is a disqualifier ([Everything Design: trust signals](https://www.everything.design/blog/trust-signals-b2b-website)); for a small vendor it's not the deciding factor, but its *absence* becomes ammunition the moment an accountant, a nephew who "knows computers", or a competitor raises doubt. It inoculates the relationship at near-zero cost.
- **dowiz implementation:** Footer line + one `/privacy` page in Albanian: "Të dhënat tuaja: në BE, kopje rezervë çdo ditë, të vetat tuajat — eksportojini kur të doni." Backups + GDPR routes already exist; this is surfacing, not building.
- **Effort:** S.

## 2. LIKEABILITY playbook — ranked tactics

### L1. Zero-to-live in one sitting (time-to-first-value)
- **Tactic:** From claim (or signup) to a live, shareable storefront with QR code in under 15 minutes — done *with* the vendor on their phone, or done *for* them (white-glove menu entry from a photo of their paper menu / PDF import, which dowiz already has).
- **Why it works:** Customers reaching first value within 14 days retain at ~80% at month 12 vs 35–50% for those who don't; no engagement in the first 3 days ≈ 90% churn odds ([SaaSmag: Time-to-Value](https://www.saasmag.com/time-to-value-saas-onboarding-retention-2026/), [Userpilot activation benchmarks](https://userpilot.com/blog/free-trial-conversion-rate/)). 57% of SaaS leaders say onboarding friction directly hits revenue; cutting onboarding investment reliably raises churn ([Digital Applied 2026 onboarding report](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/customer-onboarding-time-to-value-2026-saas-metrics-framework)). Toast wins complex restaurant accounts with white-glove setup; Square wins simple ones with 1-hour self-serve ([UpMenu: Toast vs Square](https://www.upmenu.com/blog/toast-vs-square/)) — for low-tech vendors, dowiz must be the white-glove one.
- **dowiz implementation:** "First session" checklist baked into onboarding: menu in (PDF import / photo → done-for-you), one test order placed *by the vendor themselves from their own phone* (this is the aha moment — they see the order arrive on their Viber), QR sticker PDF generated. The test-order moment is the activation event to instrument.
- **Effort:** M (assets mostly exist: PDF import, demo-builder, QR; needs choreography into one flow).

### L2. "My shop looks *good*" — pride of ownership
- **Tactic:** The storefront must make a 5-table qofte shop look like it hired a designer: their name, their colors (auto-derived palette — already built), their photos, their font (per-tenant fonts — already built), their own link/QR. It's *theirs*, not a listing inside someone else's app.
- **Why it works:** 75% of consumers judge business credibility by its website; 94% of first impressions are design ([Made For Web](https://www.madeforweb.co.uk/blog/75-of-consumers-judge-a-companys-credibility-by-its-site-design), [Zippia](https://www.zippia.com/advice/website-statistics/)). The emotional driver for the vendor is reflective delight — NN/g's third pillar: what the product lets me *say about myself* ([NN/g Three Pillars of User Delight](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/pillars-user-delight/)). On a marketplace they're row #47; on dowiz they're the whole page. This is also the built-in referral engine: a beautiful `/s/:slug` link gets shared vendor-to-vendor.
- **dowiz implementation:** Already largely built (derivePalette, per-tenant fonts, R2 photos, Maps-scraped hours/address). Add: a "shiko dyqanin tuaj" share moment at the end of onboarding (peak-end placement) + printable branded QR table-tents/stickers as a physical artifact of ownership.
- **Effort:** S (delta on existing storefront work).

### L3. Speak Albanian everywhere — UI, storefront, support, receipts
- **Tactic:** Albanian-first (not Albanian-available): admin UI, storefront, order notifications, error messages, onboarding, support, marketing. English second.
- **Why it works:** CSA Research (8,709 consumers, 29 countries): 76% prefer buying with information in their own language; 40% will *never* buy from other-language sites; 75% more likely to repurchase when customer care is in their language ([CSA Research](https://csa-research.com/l/media/Consumers-Prefer-their-Own-Language), [Slator](https://slator.com/third-global-survey-by-csa-research-finds-language-preference-of-consumers-in-29-countries/)). It works twice: the vendor's *customers* buy more from an Albanian storefront, and the *vendor* trusts a tool that speaks to them like a local, not an import.
- **dowiz implementation:** i18n catalog (al/en) already exists with a parity gate — the work is coverage + tone: every vendor-facing string reviewed by a native speaker for warmth, not machine-translation stiffness. Support scripts in Albanian.
- **Effort:** S–M (infrastructure done; needs a native-speaker copy pass).

### L4. Celebrate their wins — first order, records, milestones
- **Tactic:** Celebrate the vendor's business moments: first order ever (big in-admin moment + congratulation on their messaging channel), best-day-ever record, 100th/500th/1000th order — each with a beautiful shareable card ("Restorant X — 1,000 porosi 🎉").
- **Why it works:** Milestone celebration measurably retains: Shopify stores running milestone programs see ~28% higher retention at the critical stage and 42% higher 12-month LTV ([EasyApps: Shopify milestone celebrations](https://easyappsecom.com/guides/shopify-customer-milestone-celebrations)); Duolingo engineered streak milestones into its core retention + sharing architecture — milestone moments are natural sharing triggers that turn users into an acquisition channel ([Duolingo streak milestone design](https://blog.duolingo.com/streak-milestone-design-animation/), [Lenny's: Behind Duolingo streaks](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/behind-the-product-duolingo-streaks)). Peak-end rule: these become the *peaks* the vendor remembers about dowiz ([NN/g Peak-End Rule](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/peak-end-rule/)). And a shared milestone card is a trust signal to the *next* vendor (T5 feeding itself).
- **dowiz implementation:** Event triggers off order counts per tenant; one celebratory in-admin state + one message via their chosen channel; shareable image card (their branding, their number). Start with first-order + 100-orders only.
- **Effort:** M.

### L5. Reliability as love: the boring feature they'll brag about
- **Tactic:** Obsess over the daily loop never failing: order arrives instantly on their channel, menu edits go live immediately, the storefront never shows stale items, sold-out really means sold-out. When something breaks, message the vendor before they notice.
- **Why it works:** NN/g's delight pillars put *behavioral* (it works, fluidly, every time) beneath any surface delight — visceral polish on an unreliable product reads as lipstick ([NN/g](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/pillars-user-delight/)). Local competitors' most common complaints are delays and outdated restaurant info ([Baboon reviews](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=al.baboon)). For someone whose dinner rush depends on it, "s'më ka dështuar kurrë" ("it has never failed me") is the highest form of love — and the exact sentence that travels by word of mouth (the channel from §0).
- **dowiz implementation:** Already the engineering center of gravity (pg-boss retry/DLQ, honest health, WS authz, reliability-gate loop). Product-side delta: vendor-facing failure honesty — if a notification bounces, retry through the fallback channel and surface it in admin, never silently drop.
- **Effort:** S product delta (ongoing engineering discipline).

### L6. Micro-interactions where the money is
- **Tactic:** Spend polish budget on the few daily-loop moments: new-order arrival (sound + visible pulse the vendor can *feel* across the counter), one-tap accept, satisfying status transitions, end-of-day summary ("Sot: 23 porosi, 41,300 lekë"). Skip decorative animation elsewhere.
- **Why it works:** Micro-interactions are trigger-feedback pairs that make an interface feel acknowledging and alive ([NN/g via emotional-design corpus](https://www.nngroup.com/topic/emotional-design/?asset=articles)); the peak-end rule says to concentrate delight at the most intense moment (order arrival = money arriving) and the end (daily summary) ([NN/g Peak-End Rule](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/peak-end-rule/)). The daily summary doubles as the habit loop's reward — the vendor opens dowiz at close-of-day the way they count the till.
- **dowiz implementation:** Audit the order-arrival → accept → complete path with the existing critique/motion skills; add end-of-day recap (in-admin + optional Viber message). Respect reduced-motion; keep it fast on cheap Androids.
- **Effort:** M.

### L7. Meet them on the phone they own
- **Tactic:** Everything vendor-facing must be excellent on a low-end Android over patchy mobile data: admin usable one-handed during a rush, storefront fast on 3G/4G, notifications through Viber/WhatsApp/Telegram rather than requiring an installed app.
- **Why it works:** Mobile is >52% of global traffic and effectively 100% of this segment's computing ([Zippia](https://www.zippia.com/advice/website-statistics/)); WhatsApp/Viber-first commerce is how emerging-market SMBs already operate — ~80% of emerging-market SMBs run customer communication through messaging apps ([Wapikit](https://www.wapikit.com/blog/global-whatsapp-business-statistics-2025), [Sadu Capital on WhatsApp commerce](https://www.sadu.vc/thoughts-blog/e-commerce-through-whatsapp-how-different-markets-are-evolving)). A tool that assumes a laptop, an email address, or an app store visit filters out exactly this market. dowiz's channel-based order flow (no vendor app required) is a structural likeability advantage — the product lives inside an app they already love.
- **dowiz implementation:** Performance budget for `/s/:slug` and `/admin` on mid-tier Android/3G; keep messaging channels the primary notification surface; PWA (sw.js exists) as the upgrade path, never the requirement.
- **Effort:** M (budget + audit; architecture already aligned).

### L8. Make them smarter, gently
- **Tactic:** Once data accumulates, surface one friendly, actionable insight at a time in plain Albanian: "E premtja është dita juaj më e fortë," "Pica Margherita = 30% e porosive." Never a dashboard wall.
- **Why it works:** This shifts dowiz from "order pipe" to "partner who helps me grow" — reflective delight again ([NN/g](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/pillars-user-delight/)) — and mirrors what vertical-SaaS winners like Slice sell: not software, but "built to run and grow pizzerias" ([Slice](https://slice.com/why-slice/)). Insights are also natural conversation starters for the rep relationship (T4).
- **dowiz implementation:** One "insight card" on the admin home, rotating weekly, computed from existing order data. No new analytics surface.
- **Effort:** M. (Do last — worthless before there's order volume.)

## 3. Do these 8 things first — mapped to dowiz surfaces

| # | Move | Surface(s) | Impact | Effort |
|---|------|-----------|--------|--------|
| 1 | **Productize the claimable demo** as the acquisition motion: pre-built `/s/:slug` + "Claim it free" CTA + ≤3-field phone-based claim | Storefront + onboarding | Very high — collapses time-to-value to zero and *is* the pitch | S–M |
| 2 | **Albanian-first copy pass** (native-speaker warmth) across admin, storefront, notifications, landing; Albanian support scripts | All surfaces | Very high — 76% prefer own language; 40% never buy otherwise | S–M |
| 3 | **Cash-first + 0%-commission positioning**: "Porositë online, paratë cash" + lekë savings calculator vs marketplace commission | Landing + pricing page | Very high — hits the #1 pain and the #1 fear at once | S–M |
| 4 | **Viber/WhatsApp human support line + founder face** on landing, admin footer, and printed QR kit; in-person setup for early vendors | Landing + admin + field ops | Very high — relationship *is* the trust layer in this market | S (ops M) |
| 5 | **"Pa rrezik" block**: free start · no card · no contract · one-tap full data export · open-source no-lock-in, on landing + pricing; wire the export button in admin | Landing + pricing + admin | High — risk-reversal stack, each element cheap, credibility multiplicative | S–M |
| 6 | **One-sitting onboarding choreography**: photo/PDF menu → live storefront → vendor places a test order from their own phone (aha) → branded printable QR | Onboarding + admin | High — first-value inside 15 min is the strongest retention lever | M |
| 7 | **First-order + 100-orders celebration** with shareable branded card + message on their channel | Admin + comms | Medium-high — retention (+28%/+42% analogues) and a self-feeding proof loop | M |
| 8 | **3 named local case studies + `/status` page + review-reply discipline** | Landing + status + external reviews | Medium-high — peer proof (73% crucial) + transparency; grows with each vendor | M (fieldwork) |

**Sequencing note:** 1–5 are launch-blocking for any Albanian go-to-market (they define the first impression); 6 unlocks retention; 7–8 compound over the first ten vendors. Everything above deliberately avoids: forced digital payments, email-first anything, compliance walls, English-first UI, and self-serve-only onboarding — each of which is a trust-killer for this specific segment.
