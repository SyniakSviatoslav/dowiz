# Food-delivery market brief — 2026 (in-house public-source pass)

First-pass synthesis of public discussions + research on the food-delivery market, framed for dowiz's
direct-ordering thesis. Public sources only (WebSearch); no scraping infra, no PII. A deeper repeatable
sweep is the STORM/Scrapling out-of-band pilot (`storm-scrapling-pilot.md`).

## The thesis dowiz is built on — VALIDATED by the market
- **Aggregator commissions are punishing:** restaurants pay **15–30%** per order, **effectively 30–40%**
  once processing + promo + refunds compound. [Rezku, Inkwood]
- **Direct ordering is winning:** restaurant-to-consumer (direct/branded) platforms held **68.75% of the
  online-delivery market in 2025** — driven by restaurants wanting to keep pricing, the customer
  relationship, and margin (avoid the 30% cut). **33%** of diners prefer ordering via a restaurant's own
  app, and direct becomes profitable after only a small % of orders shift off aggregators. [Inkwood, Toast]
- **Regulators are capping commissions** (NYC permanent 15% delivery / 5% non-delivery; SF, Denver,
  Seattle, DC) — structural tailwind for the direct model. [Rezku]
- Market is large + growing: US online-delivery ~$31B (2026) → ~$62B (2034), ~9% CAGR; DoorDash ~56%,
  Uber Eats ~23% of the US. [Inkwood, Fortune]

→ **dowiz's wedge is the validated winning model:** the restaurant's *own* commission-free ordering
channel (keep customers + margin), not another aggregator.

## dowiz's actual market — Albania / Balkans
- Albania online-delivery growing **~12.7%/yr → ~$120M by 2029**; grocery delivery +15.3% in 2026. [Statista]
- **Aggregators are entering hard:** **Glovo** dominates Tirana; **Bolt Food** growing; **Wolt**
  (DoorDash-owned) launched Mar 2025 with 130+ venues in Tirana and **expanded to Durrës** — exactly
  where dowiz's anchor tenant (Dubin & Sushi, Durrës) operates. [Balkanweb, SeeNews, Nomada]

→ **Competitive read:** well-funded aggregators (Glovo/Wolt/Bolt) are taking the Albanian market AND the
30% commission with them. dowiz's pitch to a restaurant is sharp and timely: *"Wolt/Glovo take ~30% and
own your customer — dowiz is your own ordering page: keep both."* The wedge is strongest NOW, before
aggregator lock-in deepens.

## Implications for the MVP (ties to the ship plan)
- **Lead positioning with margin + ownership** ("0% commission, your customers, your brand") — the
  message the market data backs.
- The **free-delivery nudge** (shipped) + **weekly-order-count** social signal (planned) are the kind of
  conversion/credibility features aggregators use — cheap parity wins.
- Onboarding speed matters: aggregators onboard restaurants fast; dowiz's menu-first onboarding (already
  built) is the counter — get a restaurant live on its own page in minutes.
