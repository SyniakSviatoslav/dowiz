# Trademark & Brand Policy

The source code of this project is licensed under **AGPL-3.0-only** (see
[`LICENSE`](./LICENSE)). Copyright law and trademark law are separate: an
open-source **code** licence does **not** grant any rights to the project's
**names, logos, or brand identity**. This document states what you may and may
not do with the brand.

> **Status of the marks.** The name **"DeliveryOS"** is currently used as a
> descriptive project name. It is **weak and largely descriptive** ("delivery"
> + "OS"), and is **not** a registered trademark. The permanent public brand
> name is an **open brand decision (TODO)** — do not treat any name in this
> repository as final. See [Open questions](#open-questions).

## 1. What the code licence gives you

AGPL-3.0-only lets you use, study, modify, redistribute, and **self-host** this
software, including for commercial purposes, provided you comply with the
licence (notably the source-availability obligations, including §13 for
network use). Nothing in this trademark policy narrows those code rights.

## 2. Self-hosting — allowed

You **may**, without asking:

- Run your own instance for yourself or your organisation.
- Modify and redistribute the source under AGPL-3.0-only.
- Refer to the project by name **factually** — e.g. "Powered by DeliveryOS",
  "a fork of DeliveryOS", "compatible with DeliveryOS" — as long as you do not
  imply endorsement, affiliation, or that your build is the official one.
- Keep the existing brand assets in a build **you run for yourself**.

## 3. Brand in a hosted service you sell to others — not allowed

You **may not**, without written permission:

- Operate a **hosted / SaaS offering for third parties** under the project's
  name or logo, or any name/logo confusingly similar to it. Running a modified
  copy as a service to other people is exactly the case AGPL-3.0 §13 was written
  for — you must release your source, **and** you must do it under **your own
  brand**, not this project's.
- Use the name or logo in a way that suggests the project's maintainers endorse,
  sponsor, or are responsible for your service.
- Register, or attempt to register, the project's name or a confusingly similar
  name as a trademark, domain, app-store listing, or social handle.

If you fork and offer a competing hosted service — which the licence permits —
**rename and rebrand it.** Remove the project's name and logo from user-facing
surfaces and replace them with your own.

## 4. Logos and visual assets

Brand assets (wordmark, logo, icon set, the per-tenant default theme presented
as the project's own identity) are **not** covered by the code licence. Do not
reuse them to represent a different product or a third-party hosted service.
Per-tenant brand tokens supplied by **your own** tenants are theirs, not ours.

## 5. Nominative / fair use

Ordinary factual reference is always fine: naming the project in
documentation, articles, comparisons, talks, and interoperability statements.
This policy restricts **brand impersonation**, not honest speech.
